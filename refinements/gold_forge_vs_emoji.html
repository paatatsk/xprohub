Gold Forge vs. Native Emoji

The icons on the left ship today. The icons on the right are what XProHub's category set could be — drawn in the same hand as the Welcome screen Lock, Star, and Bolt.

Claude Design · Day 2 · A comparison, not a build proposal
01 — LANGUAGE

A single hand, already in the app.

Gold #C9A84C 1.6px stroke on a transparent ground. 20×20 grid. Simple geometry — no shading, no second color, no gradient. Where the icon names a heart — a paw pad, a paint blob, the rivet of a saw, the “on” signal of a screen — one filled gold dot catches the light. Pure-energy icons (the bolt) carry no catch; they are the catch. This is the vocabulary the Welcome screen has been holding for us since launch.

04 — THE RULES

What every Forge icon agrees to.

Five rules. Not negotiable per-icon — if the rule breaks, the icon's wrong, not the rule.

Grid
20 × 20 viewBox. Renders at 26px on Home tiles, 24px in dense rows, 48–64px in feature contexts. No off-grid anchor points.
Stroke
1.6px in #C9A84C. Always round caps, always round joins. No variable weight, no double stroke, no taper.
Fill
Outline shapes are always transparent. Solid fill is reserved for the single catch dot — never for the icon body.
Catch
At most one filled gold element per icon, placed at the object's named heart. Pure-energy objects (bolt) carry no catch. No exceptions, no two-dot icons.
Geometry
Editorial, not illustrative. Drawn from primitives — lines, rectangles, ellipses, simple paths. No anatomy, no shading, no perspective.
No
No second color. No emoji glyphs alongside. No shadow, no glow, no inner highlight. No filled backgrounds behind the icon. No animation on rest.
05 — SCOPE

Twenty icons. What it costs, where it fits.

The honest read. Effort is a design-bandwidth question, not an engineering one — integration is swapping the iconForSlug() map's emoji strings for SVG component references. That's hours, not days.

Design work
1–2 sessions. Six are drawn here; the other fourteen (Errands, Childcare, Eldercare, Moving, Tutoring, Coaching, Personal Assist, Vehicle, Handyman, Gardening, Recycling, Events, Plumbing, HVAC) land in the same language at roughly 30–40 minutes each, plus review iteration.
Engineering
Half a day. Replace the two iconForSlug() maps in index.tsx and post.tsx with a single component that renders the right SVG. No asset pipeline, no font loading.
Real risk
Legibility at 26px on hardware. What looks balanced in the IDE may dim on a hot iPhone screen, or in a dark room, or for users with reduced contrast settings. That's an unknown until we test on device.
Smaller risk
Some categories don't have an obvious geometric anchor — Personal Assistance, Events, Childcare. The Forge language is geometric; if we can't draw it without anatomy, the category needs renaming, not the icon needs anatomy.
Recommendation
v1 — conditional on a hardware test of these 6 first. Same gate Receipt earned: print these six to the device, look at them in a dark room, in sunlight, and beside the emoji version. If they hold up, commission the remaining fourteen. If even one fails legibility, log Gold Forge to v1.1 and ship v1 with the glyph swaps already committed.

The lighthouse standard isn't “ship custom icons because they're better in theory.” It's “ship them because the device says so.”
